About me

I am a 2nd year Ph.D. student in Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Weiyang Liu and Prof. Yu Cheng. Before joining CUHK, I received my bachelor’s degree in Artificial Intelligence from Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU), where I was honored with the SJTU President’s Award and Outstanding Graduate of SJTU.

During my undergraduate studies, I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Yonglu Li and Prof. Quanshi Zhang. Recently, I am fortunate to be working closely with Ruobing Xie, Senior Researcher at Tencent.

My research interests

  • LLM Pre-Training and Model Structure
  • Model Interpretability
  • Training Dynamics

Publications

Lu Chen, Yuxuan Huang, Yixing Li, Dongrui Liu, Qihan Ren, ShuaiZhao, Kun Kuang, Zilong Zheng, Quanshi Zhang (2026). Can LLMs Reason Soundly in Law? Auditing Inference Patterns for Legal Judgment. ICLR 2026.

Link to ICLR

TL;DR: We present a method to evaluate the alignment between the decision-making logic of Large Language Models (LLMs) and human cognition in a case study on legal LLMs.

Yixing Li, Ruobing Xie, Zhen Yang, Xingwu Sun, Shuaipeng Li, Weidong Han, Zhanhui Kang, Yu Cheng, Chengzhong Xu, Di Wang, Jie Jiang (2026). Transmamba: Flexibly switching between transformer and mamba. AAAI 2026.

Link to arxiv Link to AAAI

TL;DR: We propose TransMamba, a novel sequence-level hybrid framework that unifies Transformer and Mamba by sharing parameter matrices and memory converter. Extensive experiments demonstrates that TransMamba achieves superior training efficiency and performance compared to single and hybrid baselines.

Xiaoyang Chen, Xinan Dai, Yu Du, Et.al (2025). DeepMath-Creative: A Benchmark for Evaluating Mathematical Creativity of Large Language Models. arxiv.

Link to arxiv

TL;DR: DeepMath-Creative introduces a benchmark and evaluation criteria for mathematical creativity, concluding that current LLMs rely more on learned-pattern recombination than genuine creative insight.

Yixing Li, Ruobing Xie, Xingwu Sun, Yu Cheng, Zhanhui Kang (2025). Continuous Speech Tokenizer in Text To Speech. NAACL 2025.

Link to NAACL

TL;DR: We propose a simple yet effective framework for continuous speech tokenizer and a text-tospeech model based on continuous speech tokens, which shows greater performance and better information preservation rate.

Yixing Li, Yuxian Gu, Li Dong, Dequan Wang, Yu Cheng, Furu Wei (2025). Direct preference knowledge distillation for large language models. arxiv.

Link to arxiv

TL;DR: We propose a novel LLMs knowledge distillation framework named direct preference knowledge distillation and demonstrate the effectiveness of preference and implicit reward in the KD process.

Xingwu Sun, Shuaipeng Li, Ruobing Xie, Et.al. (2025). Scaling Laws for Floating Point Quantization Training. ICML 2025.

Link to arxiv Link to ICML

TL;DR: This paper examines floating-point quantization in LLM training, proposes a unified scaling law, and offers key recommendations for improving model performance and cost-efficiency.

YongLu Li, Xiaoqian Wu, Xinpeng Liu, Et.al (2024). From isolated islands to pangea: Unifying semantic space for human action understanding. CVPR 2024.

Link to arxiv Link to CVPR

TL;DR: We build a verb-taxonomy action semantic space to align existing datasets into a unified ‘Pangea’ label system and propose a physical-to-semantic model that leverages it, delivering consistent improvements, strongest in transfer learning.
